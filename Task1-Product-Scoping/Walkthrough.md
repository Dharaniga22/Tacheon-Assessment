# Walkthrough: Marketing Performance Hub



## Introduction



This document explains my thinking process while defining the Marketing Performance Hub product.



The objective was not to design a complex marketing platform, but to identify the simplest solution that could effectively answer the question:



> How is our marketing performing across channels right now, and where should we focus next?



## Understanding the Problem



The first thing I noticed was that the problem was not related to data availability. Marketing data already exists in multiple platforms.



The real challenge is that analysts spend time collecting data manually, combining information from different sources, and preparing reports. This creates delays, inconsistencies, and dependency on specific individuals.



As the number of clients increases, this process becomes difficult to scale.



## Identifying the Primary User



I selected marketing analysts as the primary users because they are directly involved in reporting and performance analysis.



Although account managers and marketing managers may also use the tool, analysts experience the biggest pain point today and therefore receive the highest priority in Version 1.



## Why a Dashboard?



I considered different approaches before deciding on a dashboard.



A dashboard provides a centralized location where users can quickly understand performance without opening multiple tools. It also allows performance trends and channel comparisons to be displayed visually, making the information easier to interpret.



Another reason for choosing a dashboard is that it does not require changes to existing workflows, which was one of the constraints provided in the assessment.



## Scope Decisions



I intentionally kept Version 1 focused.



The goal is to answer the primary business question quickly and consistently rather than building every possible feature.



Version 1 includes:



* KPI visibility
* Channel comparison
* Trend monitoring
* Basic insights
* Report export capability



Features such as AI-generated recommendations, forecasting, and client-facing portals were excluded because they add complexity without directly solving the immediate problem.



## Data Considerations



The proposed solution uses data from marketing platforms already used by the team, such as:



* Google Ads
* Meta Ads
* LinkedIn Ads
* Google Analytics 4



This ensures adoption is easier because teams do not need to change existing processes.



## Defining Success



I believe the solution is successful if users can:



* Access marketing performance quickly
* Reduce manual reporting effort
* Generate more consistent reports
* Respond to client questions faster



The tool should help users move from collecting data to acting on insights.



## Final Thoughts



Throughout this task, I focused on keeping the solution practical and realistic rather than feature-heavy.



My approach was to solve the core reporting problem first, establish trust in the data, and create a strong foundation that can support future enhancements such as AI-driven insights and forecasting.
